How We Reduced Churn
by 34% in 60 Days

May 26, 2025
CornerStone Team
Retention SaaS Growth

The Problem: Great Signups,
Weak Retention

After launching on Product Hunt, thousands of users signed up for CornerStone — but many never stuck around. We realized that having a great top-of-funnel wasn't enough if users didn't find value within their first session.

Churn was sitting at 12% monthly, and most drop-offs happened within the first 5 days. We knew we had to pivot our focus from acquisition to long-term stickiness.

"I loved the idea, but didn't know what to do once I signed up."
— Former user

We weren’t just losing users — we were losing potential advocates.

Step 1: Talk to Churned Users

We started with what we had: feedback. We reached out to those who left to understand the "why" behind the data.

  • • 15 user interviews conducted in 2 weeks
  • • Most hadn't completed the initial setup
  • • Several were unaware of our best features
  • • Others just forgot to come back
Takeaway: We didn't have a product problem. We had a clarity problem.

Step 2: Redesign the Onboarding Journey

We made one clear goal: Show value in under 2 minutes. We stripped away the fluff and focused on the "Aha!" moment.

  • • Introduced a simple 3-step setup flow
  • • Embedded product tours and checklists
  • • Added contextual tooltips, not generic walkthroughs
▶ Video Demo: See the New Onboarding in Action

Result: 15% drop in churn within 3 weeks.

Step 3: Smart Lifecycle Emails

Instead of static welcome emails, we created behavior-triggered sequences that respond to what users actually do:

Didn't finish setup?

→ Get a nudge

Invited a teammate?

→ Get collaboration tips

Each email had one action, one value, and zero fluff.

↑ 61%
Open Rate
↑ 34%
CTR
↓ 9%
Inactive Churn

Step 4: Automate Success Checkpoints

We set up internal milestones inside CornerStone to track user progress. When a user stalled, we didn't wait for them to ask for help.

"It just knew when I needed help — and offered it right away."
— Product Manager, Early Adopter

The Results (After 60 Days)

Metric Before After Change
Monthly Churn 12% 7.9% ▼ 34%
Daily Active Users 1,800 2,470 ▲ 37%
NPS Score 24 41 ▲ +17 pts

Key Takeaways

  • 01

    Onboarding isn't just a flow — it's your first impression. Make it count.

  • 02

    Emails must match user behavior, not just arbitrary time triggers.

  • 03

    Success checkpoints proactively help before users ask for help.

  • 04

    Talking to churned users reveals the blind spots data can't show.
